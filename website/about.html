About NCHS Men's XC

From Left to Right: Baganz, Couch, Stack, Ashton, Wandke

Head Coach David Ashton

Coach Ashton has been with Naperville Central High School Cross Country since 2005-06, all but 2 years of that time he served as Head Coach. Through the first 7 years, he additionally coached Distance Track and Field. During his Junior and Senior years in high school he ran Cross Country and Distance Track for Downers Grove South, under Kevin Keogh (a former NCHS Asst. Coach). Following his short high school career, Coach Ashton competed for North Central College under the guidance of Al Carius and Frank Gramarosso. Throughout his coaching career he has been gifted the opportunity to work with large groups of very dedicated young men. Amongst those, many have been All-State and State qualifying athletes and teams. Most importantly they learned to love the sport of distance running and their teammates. Cultivating “Manhood, Brotherhood and a Quest for Personal Bests”, have been the focus of his time with Naperville Central.

Asst. Coach Steve Stack

Coach Stack has been with the Redhawks XC staff as an assistant coach since 2012, working specifically with freshmen runners and developing the strength program for the team. Since he has joined, the Redhawk runners have qualified as a team to the IHSA State meet six times and have qualified individuals to the state meet twice. In the spring, Coach Stack is involved with the Naperville Central Boys’ Track and Field team. Coach Stack serves as the Head Coach while focusing on the pole vault. Since then four Redhawks have qualified for the IHSA State meet pole vault. Coach Stack was a student-athlete at North Central College, where he earned a degree in History/Social Science and a minor in Secondary Education. In his senior year he finished 5th in Pole Vault (PR - 16'3.25") at the NCAA DIII Outdoor National Championships in 2010 along with a team National Championship. Coach Stack is a teacher in the Social Studies Department at Central and teaches US History, American Studies, and Sociology. When he is not at school, he enjoys eating and traveling with family: #MrsCoachStack, #MissCoachStack (Lillian), and #CoachStackJr (Simon).

Asst. Coach Grant Baganz

Coach Grant Baganz started coaching at Naperville Central in the 2011-2012 school year as the head distance coach for track and field, and as an assistant boys cross country coach. In track and field, Baganz has coached distance Redhawks to State 12 times, including All-State performances in the 800 in 2017, and the 4 x 800 and 1600 (3rd place, which is the highest finish in this event in school history) in 2018. In 2019, the 4 x 800, and 800, both won State championships, the first State championships for Naperville Central boys’ track and field since 1990. Since 2015, the Redhawks have had between 23 and 35 guys sub-5 minutes for 1600 meters each year and in 2019, the Redhawks finished #1 in Illinois and #3 in the US for the Sub-5 Challenge. During Coach Baganz's tenure, school records have been set in the 4 x 800, 800, and 1600. Coach Baganz is in his 22nd year of coaching track and field and/or cross country, including experiences as a head coach in both spots He has coached athletes to State over 70 times in track and field, including 27 total All-State performances in numerous events for both boys and girls. Baganz has also ran and/or coached at the University of Wisconsin-Parkside, the University of Wisconsin-La Crosse, and Christ Lutheran Academy in Plainfield. As an athlete, Coach Baganz’s personal records include 51.0 for 400, 1:52.9 for 800, 3:51.9 for 1500, 4:09.8 for an indoor mile, 15:08 for 5000, and 26:01 for a cross country 8K. Coach Baganz was a state champion for Racine Lutheran High School in the 800. He placed third at the state meet in the triple jump, and 9th in the 400. He also qualified in the 200 and relays. During his senior year, the team was State runner-up. Baganz also played basketball 4 years and football for 1 year. Baganz went on to compete for the University of Wisconsin-Milwaukee in track and field, and trained with the cross country team. He received his coaching certification from the University of Wisconsin-Parkside, and his master's degree in Exercise Science—Human Performance from the University of Wisconsin-La Crosse, where he also had an emphasis in Sport Administration. In 2012 he finished 7th in the USATF Masters Outdoor Championships 800 meter run and was on the US Masters Performance List for 1600 meters. Coach Baganz currently teaches K-8 Physical Education at Bethany Lutheran School in Naperville. He was born and raised in the great state of Wisconsin and currently lives in Naperville with his wife and two young sons. In addition to Wisconsin sports, Baganz loves summer, traveling, and spending time with family and friends. He loves and appreciates what he does and feels very blessed to work at great schools with tremendous support and wonderful people.

Asst. Coach Donna Wandke

Coach Wandke has volunteered with Naperville Central Cross Country since 2012. In 2015, she began coaching and attending practice everyday. She works closely with the developing runners that have goals of running sub 21 for their 3 mile race by the end of the season.

Her running experience began in junior high and then in high school at Ridgewood High School in Norridge, Illinois, where she was a captain, school record holder in the 300m hurdles and later a coach. She continued running at Elmhurst College, where Donna earned a Bachelors of Science in Mathematics and Athletic Training with endorsements in education. She began her career teaching mathematics at Naperville North and also serving as the assistant athletic trainer. Donna then earned a Masters in Educational Leadership from National Louis University. She continued teaching at Naperville North until having her third son at which point she decided to stay home with her boys.

Coach Wandke also served as a volunteer helping with splits and running events as needed for the NCHS Track and Field over the same years and then joined the coaching staff in 2020. She serves as a volunteer in all of her coaching roles at NCHS. Due to her dedication and commitment to NCHS both in these roles and as a member of the Naperville 203 Board of Education, she won the community volunteer representative for District 203 at the state “Those Who Excel” award ceremony.

Family is the focus of most of Donna’s time. She is married to Norm and has three boys, Kevin, Ryan and Alan. Extended family is very important to her and she makes every effort to help care for them in every way she can.

When not coaching, Donna enjoys running, biking, hiking and especially kayaking. She loves the outdoors and has camped, backpacked and paddled all over the US. Donna loves traveling and has been to Kenya, Japan, Russia and Guatemala along with many countries in Europe. Her favorite National Park is Zion where she enjoyed hiking with her family up the Narrows.

High School Sports: Track and Field, Volleyball, Gymnastics

College Sports: Track and Field - Hurdles

Asst. Coach Jeff Couch

Coach Couch has been involved with Naperville Central cross country teams since 2004. He assists the coaches with team and meet statistics. His own running history began in 2002, and he continues to participate in events from the 5K to half-marathons. His goal every year is to be faster than last year. In 2019, he assumed meet organizer responsibilities for the Midwest Distance Classic track meet. In his professional life, Coach Couch is a financial applications developer, combining his education in finance, accounting and computer science. He and Nancy are long-time Naperville residents, with their three sons all having graduated from Naperville Central. They enjoy spending their free time with their families.
